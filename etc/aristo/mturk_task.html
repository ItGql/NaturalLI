Thank you for considering our HIT! This is a data collection task in natural language understanding, in particular for textual entailment).

Instructions

  • You will be shown two sentences, and you should decide whether one sentence entails the other (⇒ or ⇐), the sentences say the same thing (⇔), or the sentences are not related (?).
  • One sentence entails another sentence if by reading only the one sentence, you think the other sentence is most likely true.
  • Two sentences saying the same thing is equivalent to one entailing the other, and the other entailing the one.
  • Otherwise, the sentences are not related. Unrelated sentences still often talk about the same topic, but neither entails the other.

Examples

0. If we know that: (a)Plastic is very harmful to the environment .
What can we say about: (b)cutting down a rain forest is most harmful to the natural environment.
Although both sentences talk about the environment, "plastic" and "cutting down trees" are not comparable.
0. If we know that: (a)Other human activities like cutting down forests , land clearing , even building roads and cities has harmful effects on the natural environment .
What can we say about: (b)cutting down a rain forest is harmful to the natural environment.
The second sentence only has information you would assume to be true from the first sentence; but, the first sentence has more information that may not be true having only read the second sentence.
0. If we know that: (a)The main source of energy for the water cycle is the sun .
What can we say about: (b)sunlight is the main source of energy for the water cycle.
These sentences have exactly the same information, they just say it differently.
